## Core Theme
**Build Next Gen AI Sales Agent**
Central challenge: Prototype a powerful AI sales agent that meaningfully integrates voice capabilities. Teams are expected to collaborate closely and to transform conceptual ideas into working prototypes. Successful projects will demonstrate a clear commercial use case, a functional demo, and thoughtful integration of voice-powered AI technology. While the core focus is on sales agents, there are no prescribed industries. Teams are encouraged to identify specific real-world market problems and design tailored solutions to address them.

## Suggested Project Ideas
(but not limited to)
1. **Website Sales Agent with Convo AI**
   Build an AI sales agent that engages website visitors in real time. The agent can talk to people who land on the sales page, answer questions, qualify interest, and guide them toward the next step. Teams can also explore visitor monitoring, page activity tracking, and lead capture based on browsing behavior.
2. **AI Sales Rep**
   Build an AI sales representative that can join lead or client calls with strong knowledge of Agora products and solutions. The agent should support discovery conversations, answer product questions, recommend relevant solutions, and help move the customer toward onboarding or sales opportunity creation. The AI Sales Rep may also transcribe the call and generate a post-call summary after the meeting, including key discussion points, customer needs, follow-up actions, and potential sales opportunities.
3. **Social Lead Generation Agent**
   Build an agent that identifies and collects potential leads from public social platforms such as Reddit, X, LinkedIn, and Facebook. The agent can detect buying signals, capture relevant contact or company information, categorize lead quality, and pass qualified prospects into the sales pipeline.

## Required Tech Stack
There is no required tech stack for this hackathon. Participants are free to use whatever tools, frameworks, or platforms they prefer to bring their concepts to life. Projects should clearly demonstrate a sales-focused AI workflow, such as engaging website visitors, supporting outreach, joining lead or client calls, generating call summaries, or identifying qualified leads from public social platforms.

Real-time voice AI is encouraged as a core interaction layer, especially for use cases involving live conversations, customer discovery, product Q&A, or sales follow-ups.

TRAE IDE is the recommended development environment for this hackathon. While not a judging requirement, participants are encouraged to use it throughout the day.

## Judging Rubrics
Evaluation takes place in two rounds. All teams participate in Round 1. The top 10 highest-scoring teams advance to Round 2 for live presentations before the full judging panel.

### Round 1 Screening
Judges review submitted code, documentation, and architecture.

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| **Sales Use Case Fit**: The project features a dedicated AI agent for sales, outreach, or lead generation workflows. | 25% |
| **Core Workflow Completion**: The project demonstrates a clear, complete end-to-end process. | 20% |
| **System Functionality**: The project works based on the submitted demo video, repository, and documentation. Judges should be able to understand how the solution runs and verify the main flow without team assistance. | 20% |
| **Data Capture, Output & Handoff**: The project successfully captures and outputs actionable sales data. | 15% |
| **Technical Implementation Quality**: The repository is well-structured and demonstrates sufficient technical depth. | 10% |
| **Submission Compliance**: The submission meets all formatting and material requirements. | 10% |

### Round 2 Live Presentations (Top 5 each Group)
Qualifying teams present their projects to the judging panel.

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---|
| **Functionality & Live Demo Quality**: The project works reliably and demonstrates a clear, complete, and stable workflow during the live demo. | 20% |
| **Sales Workflow Design & Execution**: The project demonstrates a strong, well-executed sales process. | 20% |
| **Technical Architecture & Implementation**: The system is well-designed and demonstrates strong engineering quality. | 15% |
| **Business Impact & Real-World Application**: The project solves a realistic problem and delivers tangible value to sales teams. | 15% |
| **Innovation & Differentiation**: The project demonstrates originality and goes beyond basic automation. | 10% |
| **Presentation & Pitch**: The team delivers a clear, well-structured, and compelling pitch. | 20% |

## Submission Guidelines
All teams must submit their projects through the Convo AI Club portal before 7:15 PM on May 27, 2026. Late submissions will not be considered for judging.

### How to Submit
1. Go to the Convo AI Club submission portal.
2. Navigate to the bottom-left of the page and click the Submit Project button.
3. Submit your GitHub repository link containing your project code and documentation.

### What to Include in Your Repository
Your submitted GitHub repository must contain:
* All project source code
* A README.md covering: project description, architecture overview, how the Agora Conversational AI Engine and RTC SDK are integrated, setup instructions, and any known limitations
* A demo (either a screen recording, a hosted live link, or clear instructions to run it locally)
* Your pitch deck or presentation slides

### Submission Checklist
* Include your Group # in the submission title: `[(Group #) Team Name - Project Name]` (e.g., `(1) AGOROAR - Sales Matic`)
* GitHub repository is public and accessible
* README.md is complete and covers all required sections
* Agora RTC SDK and Conversational AI Engine integrations are documented
* Demo is included or linked
* Submitted via the Convo AI Club portal before 7:15 PM
* Deadline: 7:15 PM, May 27, 2026. No exceptions.
